The Core Problem: Identity Spoofing

Financial institutions rely on voice authentication, but standard biometric models fail when targeted by physical-access replay attacks and AI deepfakes.

The Attack: A fraudster records a customer's voice or generates an AI clone, playing it through a phone speaker into a banking IVR system.

The Blindspot: Standard biometrics authenticate the *voiceprint* but cannot verify the *liveness* of the audio, allowing distorted, pre-recorded audio to bypass security.

The "Silo" Gap in Voice Security

The Biometrics Approach

Identity systems focus entirely on voiceprint matching. They assume the incoming audio is from a live human, making them highly vulnerable to synthetic injections.

The Cybersecurity Approach

Security teams build "gatekeepers" (PAD) to catch hackers based purely on microphone static, ignoring the regional linguistic context of the user.

VigilAudio's Solution: Bridging the gap with a unified architecture that simultaneously verifies who is speaking (Identity/Language) and how they are speaking (Biometric Liveness).

Engineering Objectives

1. Liveness Detection

Detect physical-access replay attacks mathematically. Train the system to recognize the acoustic artifacts of device speakers and room reverberation.

2. Regional Validation

Authenticate closely related, low-resource regional languages (Bengali, Nepali, Assamese) acting as an initial layer of identity verification.

3. Fraud Rejection

Implement Out-of-Domain (OOD) rejection to automatically drop automated bots, AI voice generators, or non-target linguistic spam.

The Secure Data Pipeline

Building a robust defense requires diverse, high-quality training data.

Data Extraction

Curated 50,000+ raw audio files acting as clean, "live" human baselines.

Standardization

Strict pipeline converting all audio to mono, 16kHz, with normalized amplitude for consistent security scanning.

Tensor Shaping

Variable-length audio transformed into uniform 4-second PyTorch tensors for rapid inference.

Dual Target Labeling

Manifests mapped with both linguistic identity and spoof-class targets for multi-task biometric training.

Simulating Physical Attacks

To catch fraudsters, we mathematically simulated their attack vectors.

Device Limits: Applied a 300Hz-3400Hz Bandpass filter to replicate the hardware limitations of a cheap phone speaker playing a stolen voice memo.

Room Acoustics: Applied Synthetic Room Impulse Response (RIR) with exponential decay to simulate a fraudster playing audio in a physical room.

Sensor Noise: Injected Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) to replicate the static hum of hijacked recording microphones.

Handling Fraud Data Imbalance

In the real world, fraudulent audio is a minority class compared to legitimate traffic.

  • Legitimate Bengali: 21,000 files
  • Legitimate Nepali: 5,000 files
  • Fraud/Spam Injection: 3,700 files

The Solution: Instead of simple data duplication (which causes overfitting), we utilize a Weighted Cross-Entropy Loss Function. The neural network is penalized up to 12x more for failing to catch a spoofing attack, forcing mathematical vigilance.

Multi-Task Learning Architecture

Shared Acoustic Extraction

We use a frozen Wav2Vec2-Large-XLSR-53 feature extractor to analyze foundational acoustics while minimizing server compute costs during inference.

Mean Pooling Strategy

Instead of analyzing single syllables, we implemented masked mean pooling across the full 4-second sequence to reliably detect persistent background spoofing static.

The Dual Security Heads

The network splits into two simultaneous sequences: an Identity Head (Validating Language) and a Biometric Head (Validating Liveness).

Production Security Metrics

EER

Equal Error Rate

In biometric security, simple accuracy is a flawed metric. We utilize the industry gold standard for Presentation Attack Detection (PAD): the Equal Error Rate.

Using ROC curve interpolation, we calculate the exact threshold where the False Acceptance Rate (letting a fraudster's spoofed attack pass) perfectly equals the False Rejection Rate (frustrating and blocking a real customer).

A lower EER denotes a stronger defense against fraud.
